Bacon-Wrapped

Description

Bacon-Wrapped Dates with Goat Cheese is a delightful appetizer that perfectly balances sweet, savory, and creamy flavors. This recipe features luscious Medjool dates stuffed with creamy goat cheese, then wrapped in crispy bacon and baked to perfection. With the sweetness of the dates, the richness of the cheese, and the smoky saltiness of the bacon, this dish is a crowd-pleaser that will impress your guests at any gathering."

To prepare this mouthwatering appetizer, start by pitting the Medjool dates and filling them with tangy goat cheese. The creamy cheese adds a luxurious texture and a delightful tang that complements the sweetness of the dates. Next, carefully wrap each stuffed date with a slice of bacon, ensuring that it is securely wrapped around the entire date. The bacon not only adds a crispy texture but also infuses the dish with its smoky flavor. Place the bacon-wrapped dates on a baking sheet and bake in the oven until the bacon turns golden brown and crispy. The heat of the oven transforms the ingredients into a harmonious blend of flavors, with the bacon becoming irresistibly crispy and the cheese turning warm and gooey. Serve these Bacon-Wrapped Dates with Goat Cheese as an elegant appetizer at your next dinner party or holiday gathering, and watch as your guests savor each delectable bite.

ingredients

  • 24 pitted dates
  • 4 ounces honey-flavored goat cheese, softened
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced fresh rosemary
  • 8 slices bacon, cut into thirds crosswise
  • 24 toothpicks
  • 1/4 cup hot honey, or as needed

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Using a paring knife, cut each date lengthwise through to the middle. Combine goat cheese and rosemary in a bowl until evenly mixed. Stuff each date with the goat cheese mixture. Wrap each date with a small piece of bacon and secure with a toothpick. Place on prepared baking sheet.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes.
  4. Turn on the broiler and place baking sheet 6 inches from heat. Broil until bacon is browned and slightly crispy, 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from oven and place on a serving platter; drizzle dates with hot honey.
